Philae

Music composed and designed by Sherif DAHROUG



""Philae" is more than an music release; it is a reverent homage to the Egyptian goddess Isis and a tribute to the indomitable spirit of ancient Egyptians who clung steadfastly to their beliefs during the tumultuous eras of late Roman and Byzantine rule (4th to 6th centuries). Through this musical opus, we invite you to embark on a profound journey into the cultural and spiritual tapestry of ancient Egypt. "Philae" First Movement : Isis Lost Her Companion This poignant movement captures the heart-wrenching loss experienced by Isis upon Osiris's departure. The music weaves a tale of her sorrow and disbelief as she roams Egypt in search of her beloved. Beneath the sheltering branches of the great Sycamore tree, dedicated to the goddess Nut, Isis finds solace and guidance. The melodies and harmonies evoke deep sorrow and a profound sense of loss, echoing Isis’s grief and the monumental nature of her mourning. Second Movement: The Sacred Barque Step into the ceremonial procession of Osiris's sacred barque, named "Neshmat." It carries his earthly vessel toward the celestial realm of goddess Nut. The music describes this solemn procession, where Isis gathers Osiris’s body and accompanies him on his final journey.
